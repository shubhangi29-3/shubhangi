Guess the Dots: Understanding the Impact of Centrality Bias on the Effectiveness of Crowdsourcing

We found out that centrality bias has a real effect on the surveys that we performed, where guesswork is expected to be partially prominent amongst the respondents. Some of this is mitigated by introducing math and strategy into the answers, but a trend is still observable. The results of this work could lead to guidelines and recommendations implementable in other survey and micro labor contexts where centrality bias could be expected.
